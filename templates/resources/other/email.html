Taming your Inbox

Your inbox can be the #1 reason you wont get any work done during the day. Lets talk about a few things that will help prevent you from getting sidetracked.

Why you shouldn't interrupt a programmer

Minimize the number of times you actually check your email.

Most emails aren't going to require immediate followup. Check your email 3 to 4 times a day and set aside a specific time and duration to do it.

Time periods I actually check and read emails

  • 8:00 AM
  • 11:00 AM
  • 1:00 PM
  • 4:00 PM

The Zero Inbox Theory

The Zero Inbox Theory is the theory that your work life will be less stressful if you are always caught up on your emails

I think this is hogwash

Maintaining a perfectly clean inbox takes way too much effort. It takes away from you actually doing your job.

Automate Your Inbox

Take advantage of Outlook's Rule engine to auto sort emails based on what you actually need to see.

I set my rules to only run when Outlook is open.

The Email Domain Rule

Move Emails that don't come from the Company you work for or your clients

This filters out most spam emails and newsletters. These definitely don't need your immediate attention.

The BCC or Distro List Rule

Move Emails that don't specifically have your email in the TO or CC lines.

This filter catches most email distribution lists and rarely need immediate action. It should be marked high priority if it does need immediate action.

The CC Rule

Emails that contain you in the CC list shouldn't require immediate attention.

The Invite and Accept Rule

Meeting invites typically do need prompt followup. Move these into a separate folder so that it is easier to see when you receive a meeting invite.

Order Matters

Outlook cares about which rule comes first. You'll actually generate duplicate emails if you aren't careful.

These rules only work if the people emailing you follow decent email etiquette.
